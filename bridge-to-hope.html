PROJECT 02

Bridge to Hope

Bridge to Hope is a student-run organization I founded to support children affected by poverty through educational, interactive, and free-of-charge workshops. The organization grew to over 50 volunteers and focused on combining service, education, and community outreach.

Overview

As Founder and Executive Director, I organized volunteers, planned workshops, coordinated outreach, and led initiatives designed to make educational resources more accessible to children. The project allowed me to combine leadership, communication, and social impact work.

Impact

Bridge to Hope developed a volunteer network of over 50 students and supported community-based service projects. One major initiative was a city-wide food drive that helped feed over 300 children in Imperial County, California. I was also chosen to speak at Boeing's Stop, Drop, & STEM workshop in Gardena, California where I guided high school students through STEM workshops.

My Role

I led the organization by recruiting volunteers, designing workshop structures, communicating with community partners, and organizing service events. I also helped shape the mission of the organization around educational equity and youth support.

Skills Used

Leadership, Volunteer Management, Community Outreach, Public Speaking, Program Planning, Communication, Education, Social Impact

What I Learned

This project taught me how to turn an idea into an organized community initiative. I learned how to manage people, communicate a mission clearly, and design programs that are both meaningful and practical for the communities they serve.
